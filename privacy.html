Privacy and measurement

Measurement should help the site improve without identifying visitors.

The lab needs aggregate traffic signals to decide which pages may need clearer wording, but it does not need visitor profiles, accounts, forms, session replay, ad pixels, or personal data.

Current status

Privacy-preserving measurement is enabled with GoatCounter. The configuration is limited to aggregate page views and referrers; individual pageviews, sessions, browser/system derivation, screen size, country/region, and language collection are disabled in GoatCounter settings.

Measurement boundaries

Manual review of aggregate trends to choose safer content improvementsForms, email capture, waitlists, or collecting reader-submitted data without separate approval

How measurement helps choose safe site updates

Find unclear pages

If a page gets visits but no follow-up navigation, the agent can propose clearer framing or a better next step.

Prioritize useful pages

If the experiment report gets more attention than the homepage, future improvements should make the report easier to scan first.

Keep claims modest

Traffic is a weak signal. It can guide iteration, but it is not proof of demand, validation, or profitability.

Current setup note

The conservative implementation uses GoatCounter, configured for aggregate page views and referrers only. Individual pageviews, sessions, browser/system derivation, screen size, location, region, and language collection are disabled.
