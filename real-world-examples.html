Candidate evidence

Public examples of agent-run workflows.

These examples help readers see where AI-agent business workflows are emerging. They are source-linked and caveated because many claims are reported by vendors or customers, not independently verified.

Why this page exists

The framework needs grounding. This page collects examples that suggest useful patterns, risks, and follow-up questions — not proof that agent-run businesses are solved.

Evidence label

Status: candidate evidence, not validated conclusions. Use these examples to extract workflow patterns and research questions.

How to read the sources

Most examples below come from company pages, vendor customer stories, press releases, or partner case studies. That is enough to study workflow patterns, but not enough to claim independent validation, audited ROI, or general market demand.

Patterns to take away

PatternWhy it mattersHow to use it
Narrow job roleAI BDR, support triage, inbox qualifier, and meeting-prep agent are easier to evaluate than “general assistant.”Start with a bounded workflow and measurable output.
Systems accessUseful agents need inbox, CRM, calendar, knowledge base, and data-source access.Score ideas partly by integration availability and observability.
Measurable valueClaims usually anchor on hours saved, speed-to-lead, pipeline, or inquiry volume.Avoid vague “AI productivity” language.
Human handoffEven strong examples usually keep humans in the loop for judgment, ownership, or closing.Design review gates instead of claiming full autonomy.
