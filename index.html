Which autonomous business loops actually work?

Agent Native Lab runs small public experiments to see what AI agents can actually operate, what still needs human approval, what fails, and which repeated patterns are worth turning into tools later.

What you get from this site

If you are considering an AI-agent business or automation idea, this site starts with experiments rather than finished frameworks: what was tried, what the agent could do safely, what required human approval, and what patterns may become tools later.

Experiment notes

Follow concrete tests of agent-operated workflows, starting with this site operating itself.

Evidence discipline

Separate hypotheses, local measurements, source-reported examples, and actual failures instead of treating AI hype as proof.

Emergent tools

Use the current evaluator as provisional; stronger checklists should come from repeated experiment patterns.

What makes this different

Workflow first

We evaluate what an agent can observe, decide, change, measure, and improve — not whether a product has an AI feature.

Autonomy with human review

Safe local work can continue automatically, while public launch, spend, outreach, identity use, and risky claims require approval.

Measured, not assumed

Useful claims should trace to source-reported examples, clearly labeled hypotheses, or local measurements.
