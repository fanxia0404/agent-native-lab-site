Real experiments showing what AI agents can and can’t operate.

Agent Native Lab helps founders and builders evaluate small business workflows that AI agents may be able to handle under clear limits, using local evidence: what the agent did, where humans were needed, and what failed.

Active experiment

Can an AI agent safely draft, check, and publish small updates to this site?

The first experiment asks whether an agent can safely keep this website up to date: read aggregate traffic, suggest small wording or navigation changes, run checks, open reviewed public pull requests, verify the live site, and roll back known breakages.

Current question: can the site make small, reversible improvements from aggregate traffic without overclaiming or needing routine human edits?

Read the short experiment summary, or see the full experiment report.

What the agent actually did

  • Edited public pages and private experiment docs.
  • Ran checks before publishing, including policy guard and site-specific reviewers.
  • Opened branch-protection-aware public PRs instead of pushing directly to public main.
  • Measured aggregate visits with GoatCounter.
  • Verified the live site and private-path 404 probes.
  • Added repeatable repair and rollback steps for known failures.
