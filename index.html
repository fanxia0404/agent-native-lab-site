Practical AI-agent workflow research

Which parts of a business can an AI agent run today?

Agent Native Lab helps solo founders and technical builders identify business workflows that agents can realistically operate, what still needs human review, and how to test those workflows without overclaiming autonomy.

What you get from this site

If you are considering an AI-agent business or automation idea, this site gives you a practical way to ask: can the agent observe the right inputs, take safe actions, measure results, improve over time, and know when to ask a human?

Decision support

Use the framework to decide whether a workflow is agent-ready, too risky, or still needs human operation.

Evidence discipline

Separate hypotheses, local measurements, and source-reported examples instead of treating AI hype as proof.

Reusable workflow patterns

Compare real-world examples and extract reusable patterns for agent-run work.

What makes this different

Workflow first

We evaluate what an agent can observe, decide, change, measure, and improve — not whether a product has an AI feature.

Autonomy with human review

Safe local work can continue automatically, while public launch, spend, outreach, identity use, and risky claims require approval.

Measured, not assumed

Useful claims should trace to source-reported examples, clearly labeled hypotheses, or local measurements.

Evidence level

HypothesisLocal testCandidate evidenceNo profitability claim

This is an early research summary. It is useful for framing and evaluation, not proof that agent-run businesses are solved, profitable, or validated by users.
